# NEW YORK CITY — LOCAL KNOWLEDGE BASE
# For use in Drift route generation
# Anti-tourist. Outer boroughs first. No Times Square, no Empire State, no Statue of Liberty.

## ABOUT NEW YORK CITY
New York is the most written-about city on earth, which means most people who visit see the version that exists in movies — and miss the actual city entirely. The real New York is in Jackson Heights at 3pm on a Sunday, in a Bushwick warehouse at midnight, in a Red Hook bar with no sign on the door, in the dim sum halls of Flushing that put Chinatown to shame. The tourist layer is thin but loud. One block off any major avenue, the real city starts. Locals are not unfriendly — they're just busy and they can tell immediately if you're lost. The best neighborhoods right now: Bushwick, Ridgewood, Red Hook, Jackson Heights, Crown Heights, Greenpoint, Bed-Stuy, Astoria. Avoid: Times Square, Midtown (unless specific reason), South Street Seaport tourist trap zone.

## NEIGHBORHOODS

### BUSHWICK (Brooklyn)
New York's street art capital and creative engine. Former industrial area turned artist hub — warehouses converted into studios, galleries, bars, music venues. The Bushwick Collective covers entire city blocks in murals. Gets more gentrified every year but still has real edge. Best for: art, nightlife, coffee, Latin food from the original Puerto Rican and Dominican community that never left.

### RED HOOK (Brooklyn)
Isolated peninsula, one of the few NYC neighborhoods you can't reach by subway. Industrial waterfront, brick warehouses, a strange quiet that doesn't exist anywhere else in the city. Locals are protective of it. Home to Sunny's Bar — one of the great bars in America. Best for: wandering, waterfront views, food, feeling like you found something.

### GREENPOINT (Brooklyn)
Polish enclave turned coffee-and-loft neighborhood. Still has Polish delis, churches, butchers alongside the specialty coffee shops. Quieter than Williamsburg next door. Great streets for walking — wide sidewalks, low buildings, light. Best for: coffee, food, quiet walks, the canal.

### RIDGEWOOD (Queens/Brooklyn border)
The current "real" Brooklyn — what Bushwick and Williamsburg were 10 years ago. Working-class, mostly residential, increasingly interesting bars and coffee. The border between Brooklyn and Queens runs through it. Feels authentic because it still is. Best for: bars, walking, getting off the tourist map entirely.

### JACKSON HEIGHTS (Queens)
The most ethnically diverse neighborhood on earth — measured literally by linguists. Within 4 blocks: Nepali, Tibetan, Indian, Bangladeshi, Colombian, Ecuadorian, Mexican, Filipino communities. The food here is better and cheaper than almost anywhere in Manhattan. Best for: food tourism, street life, sensory overload in the best way.

### ASTORIA (Queens)
Historic Greek community, now extremely diverse. Has a real neighborhood feeling — people actually know each other. Museum of the Moving Image is here. Bohemian Hall & Beer Garden is a living relic. Good waterfront views of Manhattan from Astoria Park. Best for: food, beer garden, the museum, a quieter Queens vibe.

### FLUSHING (Queens)
The real Chinatown — not the tourist version in Manhattan. Flushing's Chinatown is dominated by mainland Chinese immigrants, not the Cantonese diaspora that built lower Manhattan's Chinatown. The food is completely different and largely better. Massive underground mall (New World Mall Food Court), soup dumplings, Sichuan, Shanghainese. Best for: food, specifically Chinese, Korean, and Taiwanese food at the highest level.

### CROWN HEIGHTS (Brooklyn)
Caribbean-American community (primarily Jamaican, Trinidadian, Barbadian) meets young creatives. The tension and texture here is real. Nostrand Avenue is one of the most alive streets in Brooklyn — Caribbean bakeries, roti shops, record stores. Prospect Park is nearby. Best for: Caribbean food, community culture, music.

### BED-STUY (Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn)
Brownstones, history, hip-hop origins, Caribbean roots, gentrification tension. Some of the most beautiful residential blocks in New York. Tompkins Avenue is the main strip. Malcolm X Boulevard. Best for: architecture walks, food, neighborhood feel, history.

### LOWER EAST SIDE (Manhattan)
Originally Jewish, then Puerto Rican, now nightlife. Still has traces of all its past lives — Katz's Deli, the Tenement Museum, Essex Market. After midnight it's one of the best bar neighborhoods in the city. Best for: history, food (Jewish deli staples, Taiwanese, Vietnamese), late-night bars.

### EAST VILLAGE (Manhattan)
Once punk, now expensive but still interesting. St. Mark's Place is basically a tourist street now, but the surrounding blocks have incredible density of good restaurants and bars. Tompkins Square Park is a community anchor. Best for: food, bars, walking, the remnants of counterculture.

### INWOOD (Manhattan, northern tip)
Almost no tourists ever reach it. Ancient forest, the Cloisters (medieval art museum — one of the best in the city), Dominican community that has been here for generations. Feels completely different from the rest of Manhattan. Best for: The Cloisters, Inwood Hill Park, escaping.

### GOWANUS (Brooklyn)
Industrial canal (famously toxic, now being cleaned up), massive art spaces, galleries, weird bars. The canal has a strange industrial beauty. Low-rise, lots of sky, creative energy. Best for: art, walks along the canal, gallery spaces.

## SPOTS

### ART, ARCHITECTURE & QUIRKY

**The Bushwick Collective**
- Neighborhood: Bushwick (Troutman St and surrounding blocks)
- The largest open-air street art gallery in New York. Entire building facades covered in murals by internationally known artists. Walk Troutman Street and the side streets around it. Always changing — buildings get repainted regularly.
- Vibe: artsy, photogenic, free, walkable

**Mmuseumm (Cortlandt Alley)**
- Neighborhood: Tribeca/Chinatown border (Cortlandt Alley)
- The world's smallest museum — housed in a freight elevator shaft in a Tribeca alley. Exhibits change but always focus on overlooked objects from modern life. Ring the doorbell. Genuinely strange and wonderful.
- Vibe: quirky, art, unusual, tiny

**City Reliquary**
- Neighborhood: Williamsburg (372 Metropolitan Ave)
- Obsessive community museum of NYC artifacts — subway tokens, old photographs, fragments of demolished buildings, pieces of the Brooklyn Bridge. Run by volunteers. Completely idiosyncratic and wonderful.
- Vibe: historic, quirky, local, cheap

**The Noguchi Museum**
- Neighborhood: Astoria, Queens (9-01 33rd Road)
- Isamu Noguchi's studio turned into one of the best sculpture museums in the city. Open courtyard, serene atmosphere, no crowds. One of those places New Yorkers recommend and tourists rarely find.
- Vibe: artsy, calm, architectural, niche

**Socrates Sculpture Park**
- Neighborhood: Astoria, Queens (32-01 Vernon Blvd)
- Outdoor sculpture park on the East River waterfront — abandoned landfill turned art space. Free. Changing exhibitions of large-scale sculpture. The Manhattan skyline is directly across the water.
- Vibe: artsy, outdoors, free, photogenic

**Green-Wood Cemetery**
- Neighborhood: South Slope/Greenwood Heights, Brooklyn
- Victorian cemetery from the 1830s — one of the most beautiful public spaces in New York. Rolling hills, Gothic entrance gates, 478 acres, panoramic views of Manhattan. Leonard Bernstein is buried here. Parrots live in the Gothic gate towers.
- Vibe: photogenic, peaceful, historic, unusual

**The Elevated Acre (55 Water St)**
- Neighborhood: Financial District, Manhattan
- Hidden rooftop public plaza above the Financial District — elevated garden with views of the harbor and Brooklyn Bridge. Almost no one knows it exists. Take the escalator off the street.
- Vibe: hidden, photogenic, peaceful, unusual

**The Panorama of the City of New York (Queens Museum)**
- Neighborhood: Flushing Meadows, Queens
- A 9,335 square foot scale model of every single building in New York City, built for the 1964 World's Fair and maintained since. Every street, every block, at 1:1200 scale. One of the most surreal things in the city.
- Vibe: quirky, historic, unusual, architectural

**Pioneer Works**
- Neighborhood: Red Hook, Brooklyn (159 Pioneer St)
- Former ironworks factory turned into one of the best arts spaces in New York. Concerts, exhibitions, community events, a bookshop, a café. The building alone is worth the trip to Red Hook.
- Vibe: artsy, industrial, community, cultural

**Louis Armstrong House Museum**
- Neighborhood: Corona, Queens (34-56 107th St)
- Louis Armstrong lived in this modest house in Queens for 28 years until his death in 1971. The entire house is preserved exactly as he left it. Guided tours. One of the most affecting small museums in New York.
- Vibe: historic, music, unusual, emotional

**Weeksville Heritage Center**
- Neighborhood: Crown Heights, Brooklyn
- Preserved 19th-century houses from Weeksville — one of the first free Black communities in America, established in 1838. Largely forgotten until rediscovered in 1968. Important and largely unvisited.
- Vibe: historic, important, niche, emotional

**Newtown Creek Nature Walk**
- Neighborhood: Greenpoint, Brooklyn
- The industrial canal between Brooklyn and Queens — one of the most polluted waterways in America, now being cleaned up. The nature walk along it has a strange, post-industrial beauty. Huge silver digestion tanks visible across the water.
- Vibe: unusual, industrial, photogenic, weird

### CAFÉS & COFFEE

**Sey Coffee**
- Neighborhood: Bushwick, Brooklyn (18 Grattan St)
- One of the best specialty roasters in New York. In a former warehouse, very minimal, takes coffee seriously without being pretentious about it. The space is beautiful.
- Vibe: chill, artsy, serious coffee

**Devoción**
- Neighborhood: Williamsburg, Brooklyn (69 Grand St)
- Colombian specialty roaster with a stunning two-story space — a living wall of plants, skylight, Colombian coffee flown in and roasted fresh. Beautiful and the coffee is exceptional.
- Vibe: photogenic, chill, specialty coffee

**Abraço**
- Neighborhood: East Village, Manhattan (86 E 7th St)
- Tiny counter coffee shop with maybe 6 seats. The olive oil cake is famous. One of the original serious espresso spots in the city. Old school.
- Vibe: tiny, serious, old neighborhood feel

**Champion Coffee**
- Neighborhood: Greenpoint, Brooklyn (133 Driggs Ave)
- Neighborhood café that's been there long enough to feel genuinely local. Good espresso, good eggs, people reading actual books.
- Vibe: chill, local, unpretentious

**Variety Coffee**
- Neighborhood: Bushwick / multiple locations (146 Wyckoff Ave)
- Bushwick's original third-wave coffee. Multiple locations but the Wyckoff Ave spot is the one to go to. Local institution.
- Vibe: chill, local, reliable

**Think Coffee**
- Neighborhood: Greenwich Village / multiple locations
- Worker-owned and fair trade. Good coffee, ethical sourcing, student-friendly. The Village location is worth it.
- Vibe: chill, ethical, neighborhood

**Gossip Coffee**
- Neighborhood: Astoria, Queens
- Local Astoria institution with a loyal neighborhood following. Nothing pretentious — just good coffee and people who actually live nearby.
- Vibe: local, no-frills, neighborhood

### FOOD — REAL & NICHE

**Di Fara Pizza**
- Neighborhood: Midwood, Brooklyn (1424 Ave J)
- Possibly the best pizza in New York. Dom DeMarco made every single pizza himself for 50+ years. Now his kids make them. Long waits. Cash. Worth every minute.
- Vibe: legendary, foodie, pilgrimage

**Lucali**
- Neighborhood: Carroll Gardens, Brooklyn (575 Henry St)
- Tiny cash-only pizza place in a converted candy store. Often cited as the best pizza in the city alongside Di Fara. BYOB. Long waits — show up early or get on the waitlist.
- Vibe: legendary, romantic, foodie, cash only

**Russ & Daughters Café**
- Neighborhood: Lower East Side, Manhattan (127 Orchard St)
- The café offshoot of the 100-year-old appetizing shop. The best bagel and lox in New York, full stop. Smoked fish, cream cheese, the works. The original shop is a few blocks away.
- Vibe: iconic but local, historic, Jewish deli culture, foodie

**Vanessa's Dumpling House**
- Neighborhood: Lower East Side / Chinatown (118A Eldridge St)
- Iconic cheap dumpling counter. Fried sesame pancake sandwiches with beef or pork are the move. Extremely cheap, always busy.
- Vibe: foodie, cheap, local institution

**Xi'an Famous Foods**
- Neighborhood: Multiple (original in Flushing, best in LES / East Village)
- Hand-pulled noodles from Xi'an province — spicy, oily, lamb-heavy. Nothing like it. The cold skin noodles and the spicy cumin lamb burger changed how New York eats.
- Vibe: foodie, cheap, noodles, unique

**Hometown Bar-B-Que**
- Neighborhood: Red Hook, Brooklyn (454 Van Brunt St)
- Texas-style barbecue in an industrial Red Hook space. Long lines on weekends. The brisket is correct. One of the best BBQ spots on the East Coast.
- Vibe: foodie, casual, worth the trek

**Arepa Lady**
- Neighborhood: Jackson Heights, Queens (77-17 37th Ave)
- The original Arepa Lady has been making Colombian arepas on the street for decades. Now has a proper spot in Jackson Heights. The arepa de choclo with cheese is the one.
- Vibe: foodie, authentic, affordable, community institution

**Smorgasburg**
- Neighborhood: Williamsburg (Saturday) / Prospect Park (Sunday) - seasonal
- The biggest weekly food market in America — 100 local vendors every weekend. Where new NYC food trends actually start. Don't go hungry.
- Vibe: foodie, social, casual, outdoor, weekend

**Superiority Burger**
- Neighborhood: East Village, Manhattan (119 Ave A)
- Brooks Headley's cult vegetarian burger spot. Tiny, cash-friendly, constantly creative menu. The "burger" is a revelation even for meat eaters. Often has a line.
- Vibe: foodie, vegetarian, cult following, casual

**Taiwanese Treats (multiple Flushing stalls)**
- Neighborhood: Flushing, Queens (New World Mall Food Court, 136-20 Roosevelt Ave)
- The basement food court under New World Mall in Flushing. Soup dumplings (xiao long bao), scallion pancakes, stinky tofu, mango shaved ice. Cheap, no English menu, incredibly good.
- Vibe: foodie, adventurous, cheap, cultural immersion

**Katz's Delicatessen**
- Neighborhood: Lower East Side, Manhattan (205 E Houston St)
- Open since 1888. The pastrami sandwich is the best in the world. Expensive. Worth it. The ticket system, the countermen, the sawdust — all intact.
- Vibe: legendary, historic, foodie, touristy but earns it

**La Esquina**
- Neighborhood: Nolita, Manhattan (114 Kenmare St)
- Street-level taqueria hides a subterranean Mexican brasserie. The downstairs is reservation only and feels like a speakeasy. The upstairs counter is walk-in and cheaper.
- Vibe: foodie, cool, hidden

**Juliana's Pizza**
- Neighborhood: DUMBO, Brooklyn (1 Front St)
- Coal-fired thin-crust pizza under the Brooklyn Bridge. The original Grimaldi's owner opened this after a dispute. Views of the bridge. Better pizza than Grimaldi's next door.
- Vibe: foodie, photogenic, Brooklyn classic

### BARS & NIGHTLIFE

**Sunny's Bar**
- Neighborhood: Red Hook, Brooklyn (253 Conover St)
- No sign on the door. Cash only. Opened in 1890. One of the last real working-class waterfront bars in New York — barely changed in decades. The owner Sunny Balzano played guitar here for 30 years. Live music some nights. One of the great bars in America.
- Vibe: local institution, dive, live music, cash only

**Please Don't Tell (PDT)**
- Neighborhood: East Village, Manhattan (113 St. Marks Pl — enter through Crif Dogs)
- The original NYC speakeasy — enter through a phone booth inside a hot dog shop. Reservation required (phone only at 3pm). Craft cocktails that genuinely justify the concept.
- Vibe: nightlife, cocktails, experiential

**Attaboy**
- Neighborhood: Lower East Side, Manhattan (134 Eldridge St)
- No menu. Tell the bartender what you like, what you're in the mood for, your favorite spirits — they make something. Alumni of Milk & Honey, one of the originators of NYC craft cocktails.
- Vibe: cocktails, intimate, knowledgeable bartenders

**Death & Co**
- Neighborhood: East Village, Manhattan (433 E 6th St)
- One of the bars that started the craft cocktail movement. Dark, serious, excellent. Go on a weeknight.
- Vibe: cocktails, dark, serious, nightlife

**Botanica Bar**
- Neighborhood: Lower East Side, Manhattan (47 E Houston St)
- Dark, unpretentious, good cheap drinks. DJ nights. No velvet rope. Real bar.
- Vibe: dive-ish, nightlife, local, affordable

**Bohemian Hall & Beer Garden**
- Neighborhood: Astoria, Queens (29-19 24th Ave)
- The last surviving Czech beer garden in New York City — opened 1910 and still the same. Massive outdoor space. Czech beer on tap. Pierogies. Kielbasa. Feels like a different city.
- Vibe: outdoor, historic, community, beer, queens gem

**Nowadays**
- Neighborhood: Ridgewood, Queens (56-06 Cooper Ave)
- Outdoor dance party bar in Ridgewood — the real underground club scene, not the velvet rope kind. Outdoor space, great sound system, interesting crowd.
- Vibe: nightlife, dancing, underground, outdoor

**Maison Premiere**
- Neighborhood: Williamsburg, Brooklyn (298 Bedford Ave)
- New Orleans-style absinthe and oyster bar. Garden in the back. Feels like another century in another city. The absinthe drip service is theatrical and correct.
- Vibe: cocktails, romantic, theatrical, oysters

**Jimmy's Corner**
- Neighborhood: Midtown, Manhattan (140 W 44th St)
- Boxing bar run by Jimmy Glenn, trainer to Muhammad Ali. In Midtown but nothing like Midtown — cheap beer, boxing photos floor to ceiling, old regulars, jukebox. The anti-Times Square.
- Vibe: dive, historic, cheap, boxing culture

**The Clover Club**
- Neighborhood: Cobble Hill, Brooklyn (210 Smith St)
- Classic cocktail bar in a beautiful pre-war space. One of Brooklyn's best. Gin fizzes are the move.
- Vibe: cocktails, elegant, neighborhood gem

### PARKS & OUTDOORS

**Inwood Hill Park**
- Neighborhood: Inwood, Manhattan (northern tip)
- Old-growth forest in Manhattan. The last remaining natural forest in the borough — ancient trees, caves where Native Americans sheltered, a tidal marsh. Almost no tourists. Deer sometimes. 196 acres.
- Vibe: nature, escape, unusual, peaceful

**Fort Tryon Park & The Cloisters**
- Neighborhood: Washington Heights/Inwood, Manhattan
- The Cloisters is a medieval art museum built from actual pieces of European monasteries — one of the best museums in the city that most tourists never reach. Fort Tryon Park around it has panoramic Hudson River views.
- Vibe: historic, peaceful, escape, art, views

**Domino Park**
- Neighborhood: Williamsburg, Brooklyn (300 Kent Ave)
- Former Domino Sugar factory waterfront, converted into a public park. Elements of the old refinery preserved throughout. Spectacular views of Manhattan. The sugar silos are now affordable housing — you can see the skyline through giant glass walls.
- Vibe: photogenic, history, waterfront, design

**Prospect Park**
- Neighborhood: Park Slope/Crown Heights/Flatbush, Brooklyn
- Brooklyn's answer to Central Park — and honestly better. Designed by the same people (Olmsted & Vaux) and they considered it their masterpiece. Diverse crowd, great trails, waterfall, meadow, the Long Meadow.
- Vibe: escape, nature, community, weekends

**Dead Horse Bay**
- Neighborhood: Marine Park/Flatlands, Brooklyn
- Isolated beach at the southern tip of Brooklyn — the shoreline exposes artifacts from a 20th-century landfill. Old bottles, china, shoes, household objects from the 1950s wash up constantly. Archaeologically bizarre, weirdly beautiful.
- Vibe: unusual, historical, lonely, discovery

**Rockaway Beach (Beach 97th to 67th)**
- Neighborhood: Rockaway Peninsula, Queens
- The locals' beach. A90-minute subway ride from Midtown but feels like a real beach town — surf culture, boardwalk tacos, life guards, actual waves. Better than Coney Island.
- Vibe: outdoor, summer, surf, escape

**Brooklyn Bridge Park (Piers 1–6)**
- Neighborhood: DUMBO/Brooklyn Heights
- Reclaimed industrial waterfront under the Brooklyn Bridge. Best views of Manhattan from anywhere. Jane's Carousel is a historic merry-go-round in a glass pavilion. Go at golden hour.
- Vibe: photogenic, waterfront, design, views

### MARKETS & SHOPPING

**Brooklyn Flea (DUMBO)**
- Neighborhood: DUMBO, Brooklyn (80 Pearl St — weekends)
- The best flea market in New York. Vintage, antiques, records, food vendors. Under the Manhattan Bridge overhang on weekends. Rain or shine.
- Vibe: local, vintage, food, weekend

**Strand Bookstore**
- Neighborhood: Union Square, Manhattan (828 Broadway)
- 18 miles of books. One of the last great independent bookstores in New York. The dollar cart outside. The rare book room upstairs. A New York institution since 1927.
- Vibe: bookshop, local institution, cheap finds

**Housing Works Bookstore**
- Neighborhood: Soho, Manhattan (126 Crosby St)
- All-volunteer nonprofit bookstore that funds HIV/AIDS services. Beautiful space in a Soho loft. Used books, records, films. Events most nights. Buy a book, it goes to something good.
- Vibe: bookshop, nonprofit, beautiful space, community

**Economy Candy**
- Neighborhood: Lower East Side, Manhattan (108 Rivington St)
- Family-run candy shop since 1937. Floor-to-ceiling shelves of international candy, nostalgic American candy, imported chocolates. A time capsule of a business.
- Vibe: quirky, nostalgic, affordable, LES history

**Essex Market**
- Neighborhood: Lower East Side, Manhattan (88 Essex St)
- Relocated but still alive — multi-vendor market hall with local vendors, food stalls, specialty grocers. A continuation of the historic Essex Street Market.
- Vibe: foodie, local, diverse

**Queens Night Market**
- Neighborhood: Flushing Meadows, Queens (park entrance at 111th St — seasonal)
- 80+ food vendors from 80+ countries, every Saturday night in the summer. The most diverse food market on earth — literally. Small plates from $5.
- Vibe: foodie, diverse, outdoor, affordable, summer only

### CULTURE & MUSIC

**National Sawdust**
- Neighborhood: Williamsburg, Brooklyn (80 North 6th St)
- Intimate concert venue in a converted warehouse. Focus on new classical, experimental, jazz, and hybrid genres. Incredible acoustics. One of the best small venues in the city.
- Vibe: music, intimate, serious, artsy

**Bossa Nova Civic Club**
- Neighborhood: Bushwick, Brooklyn (1271 Myrtle Ave)
- Electronic music club with a real sound system and a genuine underground scene. Not a bottle service club. Small, dark, sweaty, correct.
- Vibe: nightlife, electronic, underground, dancing

**The Bell House**
- Neighborhood: Gowanus, Brooklyn (149 7th St)
- Industrial space in Gowanus with great programming — comedy, music, literary events. The kind of venue that defines Brooklyn neighborhood life.
- Vibe: live events, music, comedy, community

**Baby's All Right**
- Neighborhood: Williamsburg, Brooklyn (146 Broadway)
- Small venue with a bar in front and a music room in back. Bands before they blow up. Good drinks. No attitude.
- Vibe: live music, nightlife, casual, Williamsburg

### SPECIFIC STREETS & WALKS

**Nostrand Avenue, Crown Heights**
- One of the most vibrant streets in Brooklyn — Caribbean bakeries, roti shops, Jamaican beef patty spots, Haitian restaurants, evangelical churches, record stores. The real Crown Heights.
- Vibe: local, Caribbean, food, community

**Franklin Avenue, Crown Heights**
- The gentrifying spine of Crown Heights — coffee shops and natural wine bars alongside old-school businesses that have been there for decades. The tension between old and new is visible on every block.
- Vibe: local, neighborhood change, food, coffee

**Troutman Street, Bushwick**
- Ground zero for the Bushwick Collective. Every building surface is a mural. Walk the surrounding blocks — Wilson Ave, St. Nicholas Ave — for more.
- Vibe: street art, artsy, photogenic

**The High Line (start at 10th Ave & 34th St, walk south)**
- Elevated railway converted into a public park — genuinely worth it despite being well-known. Best in the morning. Start at the northern end, walk south to the Meatpacking District.
- Vibe: photogenic, design, walking, touristy but earns it

**Roosevelt Avenue, Jackson Heights**
- Runs under the elevated 7 train. The street below is pure sensory overload — taquerias, Nepali restaurants, Colombian bakeries, Tibetan momos, cumbia from car stereos. One of the most alive streets in New York.
- Vibe: food, diverse, chaotic, local, alive

**Bedford Avenue, Williamsburg (south of Metropolitan)**
- The section of Bedford below Metropolitan Ave that gets overlooked. Independent clothing, record stores, old diners, the first wave of Williamsburg businesses that made the neighborhood.
- Vibe: local, shopping, food, history

## PRACTICAL NOTES

**Getting around:**
The subway is the move. Outer boroughs are further than they look on a map but the J, M, L, A, C, 7 trains all take you somewhere interesting. Queens is underrated — the 7 train is the world food corridor. The G train connects Brooklyn neighborhoods without going through Manhattan.

**Timing:**
Smorgasburg: Saturday (Williamsburg) and Sunday (Prospect Park), April–November.
Queens Night Market: Saturday nights, May–October (Flushing Meadows).
Brooklyn Flea: Weekends year-round (DUMBO location).
Di Fara Pizza: Closed Monday and Tuesday.

**NYC anti-tourist rule:**
One block off any avenue in any direction is a different world. The tourist infrastructure exists on main avenues and in specific landmark zones. Everything else is the real city.
