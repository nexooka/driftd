## ABOUT PRAGUE
Prague is beautiful and over-visited. The tourist triangle (Old Town Square, Charles Bridge, Castle) is packed with selfie sticks and bachelor parties. But five minutes away from any of this, Prague becomes something else entirely — a city of hidden courtyards, absinthe bars in cellars, Czech beer culture that takes no prisoners, and neighborhoods like Žižkov where "tourist" is still a dirty word. The secret to Prague is going up (Vyšehrad, Riegrovy Sady, Letná) or going east (Vinohrady, Žižkov, Karlín, Holešovice).

## NEIGHBORHOODS

### ŽIŽKOV
Prague's rebellious, alternative heart. Named after a one-eyed Hussite rebel leader. Gritty, artsy, beer-drinking, anti-tourist. "Upper Žižkov" (near Vinohrady) is gentrified and posh; "Lower Žižkov" is the real deal — crumbling concrete, dreads, piercings, 24-hour pubs. Home to the infamous Žižkov TV Tower with David Černý's crawling babies. Some of Prague's best street art.

### VINOHRADY
The expat darling. Elegant, leafy, wine-rooted (the name means "vineyards"). Full of cool cafés, restaurants, and wine bars. English-speaking in parts, Czech in others. Jiřího z Poděbrad square is the center.

### HOLEŠOVICE
Up-and-coming industrial-turned-creative district. DOX Centre for Contemporary Art, Cross Club, art galleries in old factories. Less tourists, more locals.

### KARLÍN
Formerly industrial, now Prague's trendy food/bar district. Cobblestones, old tenements, hip restaurants, coworking spaces. Café Misto, Manifesto Market. Young professionals and creatives.

### VYŠEHRAD
Ancient hilltop fortress south of downtown. Stunning views, national cemetery Slavín, neo-Gothic church, small vineyard, original Baroque statues from Charles Bridge. Free, rarely crowded, deeply atmospheric.

### MALÁ STRANA (Lesser Quarter)
Touristy but has hidden pockets. Vojanovy Sady secret garden, Vrtba Garden, small winding streets that tourists miss if they don't explore.

### NOVÉ MĚSTO (New Town)
Central but less touristy than Old Town. Wenceslas Square is here. Good hidden bars.

### SMÍCHOV
West bank. Manifesto Andel, residential but developing.

### BUBENEČ / LETNÁ
Residential, green, home to Letná Park and its legendary beer garden with sunset views.

## SPOTS

### ART, ARCHITECTURE & QUIRKY

**Vyšehrad**
- Neighborhood: Vyšehrad (south of downtown)
- Ancient fortress hill. Stunning city views, neo-Gothic Church of St. Peter and St. Paul, national cemetery Slavín (where famous Czechs are buried), original Baroque statues from Charles Bridge, small vineyard, picnic lawns, playground, restaurant. FREE admission. Locals love it, tourists rarely come. Absolute must.
- Vibe: historic, chill, escape, photogenic, free, romantic

**Vojanovy Sady (Vojan Gardens)**
- Neighborhood: Malá Strana
- Small hidden park in Malá Strana. Quiet, swans, peaceful, picnic-worthy. Rarely discovered by tourists despite being in the touristy district.
- Vibe: chill, romantic, escape, photogenic, hidden

**Grébovka Park (Havlíčkovy Sady)**
- Neighborhood: Vinohrady
- Old royal vineyard park with a grotto, cascading fountains, gazebo with views, and a small winery. Wine tastings in summer.
- Vibe: chill, romantic, wine, photogenic, escape

**Riegrovy Sady**
- Neighborhood: Vinohrady
- Expansive park with a legendary beer garden. Panoramic sunset views over Prague. Locals drink beer here every warm evening from April to October.
- Vibe: chill, sunset, beer, social, local favorite

**Letná Park**
- Neighborhood: Letná
- Huge park overlooking the Vltava. Famous metronome sculpture on site of former Stalin statue. Beer garden with some of the best sunset views in Prague. Locals walk, jog, relax, drink beer.
- Vibe: chill, sunset, photogenic, beer, local

**Petřín Hill & Mirror Maze**
- Neighborhood: near Malá Strana
- Prague's "little Eiffel" observation tower + a Victorian mirror maze + Seminary Garden + Petřín Gardens. Funicular up the hill if you don't want to walk.
- Vibe: photogenic, quirky, historic, views

**Žižkov TV Tower + David Černý babies**
- Neighborhood: Žižkov
- Brutalist Communist-era TV tower. David Černý installed 10 giant black crawling babies climbing up it. Surreal. Observation deck at the top, restaurants, hotel.
- Vibe: unusual, photogenic, views, unique

**Cross Club**
- Neighborhood: Holešovice
- Steampunk industrial art club with pipes, wires, and a tree growing in the middle. Multiple floors with different music styles (punk, metal, electronic). Late-night food. Wednesday nights are solid. International students, locals.
- Vibe: nightlife, artsy, unique, weird, music

**DOX Centre for Contemporary Art**
- Neighborhood: Holešovice
- Biggest contemporary art gallery in Prague. Rotating exhibitions. Very off tourist trail.
- Vibe: artsy, cultural, rainy day

**Manifesto Market (Florenc / Andel)**
- Neighborhood: Florenc or Andel (Smíchov)
- Open-air food market in converted factory/shipping containers. 27 food containers + 3 bars at Florenc. Japanese yakitori, Vietnamese pho, burgers, Korean poke, Czech pancakes. Transparent igloos for winter.
- Vibe: foodie, social, chill, group-friendly

**John Lennon Wall**
- Neighborhood: Malá Strana
- Constantly evolving graffiti wall of peace and freedom messages. Touristy but worth a quick stop if you're in the area. The wall keeps changing as artists paint over it.
- Vibe: artsy, historic, photogenic, quick stop

**Zizkov Highline**
- Neighborhood: Žižkov
- Elevated walkway with street art (look for the recent bird mural). Wooden stairs lead to a boardwalk. Truly off the beaten path.
- Vibe: artsy, photogenic, hidden

**Kafka Rotating Head ("Metalmorphosis") by David Černý**
- Neighborhood: New Town (Quadrio building)
- 11-meter-high statue of Franz Kafka's head made of 42 rotating silver panels. Surreal when it's moving. Free to see.
- Vibe: unusual, photogenic, quick stop, free

**Museum of Communism**
- Neighborhood: Old Town (small side street)
- Fascinating insight into Czech communist history. Touristy but genuinely informative. Rainy day option.
- Vibe: historic, unusual, rainy day

**Rotunda of Saint Martin (Vyšehrad)**
- Neighborhood: Vyšehrad
- One of the oldest buildings in Prague, inside Vyšehrad grounds. 11th century. Nearly nobody visits.
- Vibe: historic, hidden, peaceful

### CAFÉS & DAYTIME

**Café Cloe**
- Neighborhood: central
- Cute girly brunch/café for breakfast, brunch, cake, tea, coffee, lemonade. Aesthetic.
- Vibe: brunch, chill, photogenic

**Café Misto**
- Neighborhood: Karlín
- Hip minimalist café with great coffee. Where young Prague works on laptops.
- Vibe: chill, coffee, work-friendly

**Puro Gelato**
- Neighborhood: central (Na Hrobci 410/1)
- Unique gelato flavors — lavender, carrot, brownie, basil. Not your average vanilla scoop.
- Vibe: foodie, quirky, quick stop

**Kavárna Slavia**
- Neighborhood: near National Theatre
- Historic literary café frequented by Czech writers and intellectuals for decades. Overlooks Vltava. Art Nouveau interior. Touristy but authentic — famous Czechs still come here.
- Vibe: historic, intellectual, quick stop

**Můj Šálek Kávy**
- Neighborhood: Karlín
- Specialty coffee roaster, hipster crowd, excellent beans. Local favorite.
- Vibe: coffee, chill, hip, local

### FOOD — REAL CZECH

**U Kroka**
- Neighborhood: Vyšehrad
- Traditional Czech with a twist. Tatare, game, local beer. Where Czechs take foreign friends when they want to impress.
- Vibe: foodie, traditional, cozy

**Lokál (multiple locations)**
- Neighborhood: multiple (Dlouhá, Karlín, Vinohrady)
- Modern take on Czech pub food. Excellent tank Pilsner Urquell, goulash, schnitzel, beer cheese. Locals actually eat here. The Dlouhá one has a hidden speakeasy cellar called "Beer Vault."
- Vibe: foodie, beer, traditional, local

**U Vystřelenýho Oka ("At the Shot-Out Eye")**
- Neighborhood: Žižkov
- Captures the Žižkov spirit. Scrawled graffiti, crumbling concrete, beheaded statue, clientele with dreads, tattoos, piercings. Great Czech pub food and beer. Named after the one-eyed rebel Jan Žižka.
- Vibe: local, authentic, gritty, beer, late night

**U Pinkasů**
- Neighborhood: Old Town (central)
- 15th-century pub. Traditional and specialty Czech beers. Historic, cozy.
- Vibe: historic, beer, traditional

**Pub U Modré (The Blue Pub)**
- Neighborhood: Vinohrady
- Czech beer culture at its purest. For 150+ years they've served beer at the same flat price — currently around 28 CZK per liter (about $1). No frills, just beer, bread, and time travel.
- Vibe: historic, beer, local, cheap, unique

**Hospoda U Hrocha ("At the Hippo")**
- Neighborhood: Malá Strana
- Hidden authentic pub in super-touristy Malá Strana. Locals, cheap beer, traditional food, no English menu. Blink and you'll miss it.
- Vibe: local, authentic, beer, hidden

**Café Imperial**
- Neighborhood: New Town
- Art Nouveau interior, famous for being pelted with donuts as a tradition (you can actually pay to throw donuts). Full of history.
- Vibe: historic, unusual, foodie

### BARS, NIGHTLIFE & DRINKS (Prague's specialty)

**AnonymouS Bar**
- Neighborhood: Old Town
- V for Vendetta themed cocktail bar. Servers wear Guy Fawkes masks. Dimly lit, mysterious, expensive cocktails, unforgettable service. Unmarked door in Old Town alleys.
- Vibe: cocktails, unusual, mysterious, date night, nightlife

**Black Angel's Bar**
- Neighborhood: Old Town (beneath U Prince hotel, near Old Town Square)
- Underground Art Deco cocktail bar in a 12th-century Romanesque cellar. Gothic stone walls, pre-war glamour. Inspired by Alois Krcha, legendary Czechoslovak bartender. Excellent cocktails, expensive. Often cited as best cocktail bar in Prague.
- Vibe: cocktails, historic, sophisticated, date night, hidden

**U Sudu Wine Bar**
- Neighborhood: New Town
- Massive underground Gothic cellar wine bar that goes DEEP. Multiple rooms, brick vaulted ceilings, foosball room, courtyard garden. Live DJ sets Wed-Sat. A rabbit hole.
- Vibe: wine, nightlife, social, historic

**Vzorkovna ("Dog Bar")**
- Neighborhood: New Town
- Underground maze of rooms with graffiti-covered staircase. Drinks served in jam jars. Stray dogs napping on couches. Live bands, electronic music, international students. Pure chaos.
- Vibe: nightlife, unique, weird, social, late night, cheap

**Cash Only Bar**
- Neighborhood: Žižkov
- Basement bar with NO SIGN. You have to know it exists. Czech beer, spirits, fair prices. Real speakeasy, not a tourist gimmick. Cash only (of course).
- Vibe: hidden, local, beer, cheap, authentic

**Hemingway Bar**
- Neighborhood: Old Town
- Sophisticated cocktail bar specializing in absinthe and rum. Named after Hemingway for obvious reasons. Expertly crafted cocktails. Small, intimate.
- Vibe: cocktails, sophisticated, date night, intimate

**Parlour**
- Neighborhood: Old Town
- Another top cocktail bar. Minimalist, refined, small menu but excellent execution.
- Vibe: cocktails, sophisticated, intimate

**Bugsy's Bar**
- Neighborhood: Old Town
- Classic cocktail bar with 1920s Prohibition feel. Hidden entrance. One of Prague's original craft cocktail bars.
- Vibe: cocktails, historic, sophisticated, hidden

**Green Devil's Absinth Bar**
- Neighborhood: Old Town
- Prague's oldest absinthe bar. Standard looking exterior (with a green devil for photos), interior is an antique-lover's treasure trove of quirky trinkets. Killer absinthe. You can spend hours just looking around.
- Vibe: unusual, quirky, unique, drinks, weird

**Pivovarský Klub (Beer Club)**
- Neighborhood: Karlín
- 240+ beers, six rotating taps. Beer paradise for craft lovers. Czech plus international.
- Vibe: beer lovers, casual, foodie

**Zlý časy ("Evil Times")**
- Neighborhood: Nusle
- Brewpub with impressive selection of beers on tap. Underground feel. Very local, Czech-speaking crowd.
- Vibe: beer, local, casual, authentic

**Woodoo Bar**
- Neighborhood: Žižkov
- Local pet-friendly bar with voodoo doll decor. Foosball. Locals. A very "Žižkov" experience.
- Vibe: quirky, local, casual, nightlife

**HFO (69 Bořivojova, Žižkov)**
- Neighborhood: Žižkov
- Very local Žižkov pub, real flavor of a Czech bar. Open 4pm-4am. A place to drink among locals.
- Vibe: local, cheap, late night, authentic

**Nocturno**
- Neighborhood: Žižkov (Vinohradská 14)
- Underground techno club in a former tram depot. Industrial grit, electronic beats, rooftop terrace for breathers. Gritty but approachable.
- Vibe: nightlife, techno, industrial, unique

**Cross Club (again, it's that important)**
- Neighborhood: Holešovice
- See above. THE underground club experience in Prague. Different floors, different genres. Non-commercial electronic, psytrance, techno.
- Vibe: nightlife, techno, artsy, unique, weekend

**Vinotéka Noelka**
- Neighborhood: Vinohrady (near Jiřího z Poděbrad)
- Tiny dusty wine shop/bar. All Moravian (Czech) wines. Locals stand on the sidewalk after work with full glasses. Pure Vinohrady.
- Vibe: wine, local, chill, casual, quick stop

**Vinotéka U Mouřenína / Le Bouquet Vinoteka**
- Neighborhood: central
- Wine bars for proper wine lovers. Czech and international selections.
- Vibe: wine, chill, sophisticated

**Vinohrady Beer Garden**
- Neighborhood: Vinohrady (Riegrovy Sady)
- One of Prague's most beautiful parks with beer straight from the tank. Unique taste, sunset views, locals.
- Vibe: beer, chill, sunset, local, summer

### NATURE & ESCAPE

**Divoká Šárka**
- Neighborhood: Prague outskirts (west)
- Nature reserve on the city edge. Hiking trails, cliffs, natural swimming pool. Feels like you're not in a city at all.
- Vibe: nature, hike, escape, summer

**Stromovka (Královská obora)**
- Neighborhood: near Holešovice
- Former royal deer park, now the biggest park in Prague. Trees, ponds, jogging paths, planetarium. Peaceful.
- Vibe: chill, nature, escape

**Prokopské údolí**
- Neighborhood: Prague 5 (edge)
- Nature preserve with rocks, forest, walking paths. Feels remote despite being in Prague.
- Vibe: nature, hike, escape

## NOTES FOR ROUTE GENERATION

- Prague is SMALL. You can walk from Old Town to Žižkov in 20 minutes, Vinohrady in 25. Routes can span multiple neighborhoods.
- The best anti-tourist routes: Žižkov + Vinohrady, or Karlín + Holešovice, or Vyšehrad + Vinohrady.
- AVOID routing through Old Town Square, Charles Bridge, or the Castle during the day — too crowded.
- Do route people UP to Vyšehrad, Riegrovy Sady, or Letná — tourists rarely climb hills.
- Beer is cheaper than water in Prague and better than beer anywhere else in the world. Embrace it.
- Czechs are reserved but appreciate when visitors try Czech ("ahoj" for hi, "děkuji" for thanks, "na zdraví" for cheers).
- Cash is still common in cheaper places. ATMs everywhere but beware Euronet (scam rates) — use a Czech bank ATM.
- Tipping: round up or add 10% if the service was good.
- Don't put ice in beer. Don't ask for a menu in English at U Vystřelenýho Oka. Know your audience.
- Prague is magic at night — route some evening walks through Vyšehrad, Letná, or Vinohrady's quiet streets.
- In summer, ALL routes should include a beer garden or outdoor spot.
- In winter, lean into cozy cellars, cocktail bars, and warm pubs.
